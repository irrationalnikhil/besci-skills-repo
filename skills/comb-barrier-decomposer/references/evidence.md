# Evidence base — COM-B Barrier Decomposer

All citations peer-reviewed, post-2000, verified 2026-06-12 (flags noted).

## Core framework

- **Michie, S., van Stralen, M. M., & West, R. (2011).** The behaviour change wheel: A new method for characterising and designing behaviour change interventions. *Implementation Science*, 6, 42.
  - The COM-B model: behaviour requires Capability + Opportunity + Motivation; six sub-components (physical/psychological capability, physical/social opportunity, reflective/automatic motivation). Maps onward to 9 intervention functions.
  - Type: framework paper. The model is a reasoning scaffold with wide uptake in implementation research (including LMIC health programs), not an empirical law. Present it as such.

- **Michie, S., et al. (2013).** The Behavior Change Technique Taxonomy (v1) of 93 hierarchically clustered techniques. *Annals of Behavioral Medicine*, 46(1), 81–95.
  - The downstream vocabulary: once a barrier class is confirmed, intervention directions can be named in BCT terms (expert Delphi consensus from 124 candidate techniques).

## Why the opportunity-bias warning is in the skill

- **Kremer, M., Rao, G., & Schilbach, F. (2019).** Behavioral development economics. In *Handbook of Behavioral Economics: Foundations and Applications 2*, Elsevier. **(Handbook chapter — flagged, not journal-refereed.)**
  - In low-resource settings, structural/opportunity constraints (liquidity, time, access) frequently bind before motivational ones, and behavioral deviations have amplified consequences under scarcity.

- **Bettinger, E. P., Long, B. T., Oreopoulos, P., & Sanbonmatsu, L. (2012).** The role of application assistance and information in college decisions. *QJE*, 127(3), 1205–1242.
  - The canonical demonstration that what looks like a motivation problem is often a friction problem: assistance moved enrollment ~8pp; information alone moved nothing.

## Why hypotheses-not-findings is enforced

- **Bryan, C. J., Tipton, E., & Yeager, D. S. (2021).** Behavioural science is unlikely to change the world without a heterogeneity revolution. *Nature Human Behaviour*, 5(8), 980–989.
  - Barrier structure is heterogeneous across contexts; a decomposition that was right in one district can be wrong in the next. Local validation is not optional.

- **Datta, S., & Mullainathan, S. (2014).** Behavioral design: A new approach to development policy. *Review of Income and Wealth*, 60(1), 7–35.
  - The diagnose-then-design discipline this skill enforces.

## Calibration notes

- The skill makes no effect-size claims; it produces hypotheses and discriminating questions.
- WEIRD flag: COM-B originates in UK health policy. Its *categories* travel well; its *typical barrier patterns* do not — hence the social-sector example column and the opportunity-bias warning.
